Altamont Advisory Board

About.

The Altamont Landfill Settlement Agreement created the Altamont Education Advisory Board to propose allocation of funds in the Education Account for diversion education programs, job training in the field of waste diversion and recycling and mitigating the impacts of the landfill operations on the affected neighboring community. Funds generated from the $0.34 per ton surcharge originating from the Altamont Landfill are expected to amount to approximately $500,000 annually. This per ton surcharge fee will be increased based on the consumer price index effective January 1, 2017. Additional funds are also being generated from the implementation of the tipping fee surcharge at the Vasco Road Landfill owned by Republic Services Inc.

The Altamont Landfill accepts material for disposal from Alameda County jurisdictions, and from the city of San Ramon. Funding programs in diversion education and job training in waste diversion and recycling throughout the landfill service area will preserve the life of the landfill and save resources.
